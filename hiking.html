An Old Favorite

I love hiking. I arguably shouldn't have included it in this journal, but maybe
exploring one of my all-time favorite hobbies could help me find something new
and exciting to try. I have been hiking for most of my life. It has been upsettingly
difficult to find time to hike now that I am a fulltime student with a fulltime job,
but when I do get a change to hike, I genuinely love it so much. I love having time to
enjoy the natural world around me and to get lost in my own thoughts with nothing but a
journey and a destination ahead of me. I also really love getting to see cool new sights,
so any hike with a landmark at the end is an instant favorite for me. :)

Railroad Tunnel Trail

One of my recent favorite hikes was Railroad Tunnel Trail. This is a short and sweet
hike in Payson. It was only a couple of miles, relatively flat except for some steep
hills at the end. The railroad tunnel at the end was a bit dissapointing. It was still
cool to see but I guess I was hoping it would go a bit deeper. That being said, sometimes
its about the journey, and not the destination, and the hike to the tunnel was gorgeous.
I went with a close friend in the middle of June, and the picture on the right shows how
serene and luscious the environment was. It was so nice to escape the heat of the valley
and enjoy a beautiful hike in Payson.

Crown Jewel and Takeaways

My "Crown Jewel" of hiking was a roughly 24 mile hike from one rim of the Grand Canyon
to the other. I felt so accomplished and proud of myself for completing that hike. It
was hard to enjoy it at the time, especially when comparing it to some of the hikes I
have done in Payson or Flagstaff. It's hard to enjoy the spectacular view of the canyon
when the sun is literally melting you. That being said, I brag about this hike all the time.
Despite the fact that it was not very enjoyable at the time, I loved it because I was proud
what I accomplished. Therefore, it may be beneficial for me to try and find a creative
"project-based" hobby. I don't want to be prideful, but I think I want a hobby that will
allow me to show-off a little bit. I want to find a creative hobby that I can be proud of,
or maybe another physical hobby like hiking.
