My Brother's Hobby

My oldest brother loves board games which use small miniature models. He enjoys a war
game called Warhammer and a role palying game called Dungeons and Dragons. In my effort
to try new hobbies, my brother graciously offered to teach me how to paint these miniature
models. He has some very cooling looking little figures, so I thought it could be fun to
try painting my own. The image in the bottom right is similar to a figure I tried to paint.
It is a giant rat-person for the game Warhammer.

Slow and Steady

I don't know if this may be due to my own inexperience painting models, or if I am just
not patient enough to be a painter, however, I felt like it took me ages to paint this
model. It is only a couple of inches tall, and yet it was a very time-consuming process
to paint the model. Additionally, despite my hours spent painting, my figure wasn't nearly
as nice as the one in the photo. I shouldn't expect my first attempt at a hobby to go well,
however, my model was particularly ugly despite the long hours spent painting. :)

Takeaways

It was very fun to try a hobby with my brother, however, I don't know if this hobby is
meant for me. To start, painting this model alone was a very expensive process. My
brother bought a box of around 20 of these rat-people, and the box cost him over $50.
Adding to that price was the price of brushes, paints, and glue in order to assemble
the model. This results in a very costly hobby with relatively miniature results.
This, on top of my issues with patience, makes me hesitant to continue paiting board
game models, despite the process being pleasantly enjoyable. It was also very
therapeutic to watch my plastic model come to life with each coat of paint,
I just don't know if that therapeutic feeling was worth the price we paid.
