Building Lego

One of the books I've read recently suggested trying to relearn some of the
hobbies I had when I was a kid. Supposedly it would provide insight into how
I have grown as a person and could help me find something to be passionate
about today. To try this, I bought myself a Lego brick set. I chose to build
Leonardo da Vinci's flying machine.

My Experience

I did enjoy building the Lego Set. It was fun to relearn a hobby I spent so
much of my youth practicing. That being said, it did not truly feel like a
creative hobby. I didn't conclude my set feeling like I made something new,
rather, I felt like I just spent an hour and a half following instructions.
Perhaps, when I was younger, I would have been fueled by a desire to build
giant battles between the little toy figures, but today, I merely walked away
with a cool decoration for a shelf.

Fun for an adult?

While the book I read recommended relearning a childhood hobby, I don't feel
like this experiment was very beneficial for me. I didn't discover any lost
truths about who I am as a person, or any insights into what I might enjoy
learning as an adult. Rather, I just found that I didn't enjoy the same things
I did in my childhood, and that isn't really surprising. Despite that, I am still
glad I spent the time to re-try something I haven't done in a long time, but my
hobby search must still continue.
